# Anti-slop — the named defects of generic AI UI

"AI slop UI" is not a vibe, it is a checkable list of structural habits.
design-review treats every item below as a FINDING; the layout skills treat
them as forbidden defaults. Token-swapping a slop skeleton produces branded
slop — structure is where the disease lives.

## The tells (each one a named defect)

1. **SECTION-SKELETON REPEAT** — kicker → heading → lead paragraph →
   3-column grid of equal cards, stacked N times. If two adjacent sections
   share a skeleton, one of them must be recomposed. Every section earns its
   own art direction from its content.
2. **UNIFORM DENSITY** — nothing big, nothing small; everything medium.
   World-class pages commit scale violence: a display numeral 10–15× the
   caption size beside it, a full-bleed figure next to a dense table.
   Measure it: if the largest text on a screen is < 8× the smallest, the
   composition is timid.
3. **CARD-BOXING** — content imprisoned in rounded 1px-border boxes because
   boxes are easy. Type, rules, and whitespace can structure most content;
   a card must earn its border (it's an artifact, a ticket, a document —
   something that IS a bounded object).
4. **NO GRID VIOLATION** — nothing bleeds, overlaps, rotates, or hangs in a
   margin. Print design breaks its own grid deliberately, at least once per
   spread: a rotated stamp, an image crossing a column rule, a pull-quote
   hanging outside the measure, marginalia.
5. **DESCRIBED, NOT SHOWN** — a paragraph *about* the product's output
   instead of the output itself, typeset as the artifact it is. If the
   product makes reports, a real report page appears ON the page, styled as
   a filed document — content-as-artifact.
6. **TEXT DESERTS** — three consecutive viewports with no figure,
   illustration, diagram, or artifact. Sections are inhabited (spot art,
   figures with captions, stamps, specimens) or they read as filler.
7. **FURNITURE-FREE PAGES** — no folios, no section numbering, no rules
   grammar, no figure captions, no colophon. Print furniture is what makes
   a page feel edited by someone rather than emitted.
8. **SYMMETRY AS DEFAULT** — everything centered or everything 50/50.
   Asymmetric ratios (2/3+1/3, 5/8+3/8) with intent beat mirror balance.
9. **HEDGE-COPY** — headings that could sit on any SaaS page ("Powerful
   features", "How it works" as the only voice). The brand's own vocabulary
   (here: programme, acts, ovation, doors open, filed) does the work.
10. **DECORATIVE MOTION** — animation that signals nothing. Motion is
    reserved for causality plus the ONE signature ambient move per page.

11. **INHERITED IA** — keeping the predecessor's navigation, component
    vocabulary, and page skeleton by default and restyling in place. A
    rebrand that never asks "should this even be an app bar / a card grid /
    this section order?" is decoration. Named after being caught twice:
    tokens swapped on a SaaS skeleton, then editorial furniture glued onto
    the same skeleton. Redesign the chrome from the brand's own world
    first; only then reuse what survives on merit.

## The review question
Hold a screenshot at arm's length next to the board: could this layout be
any product's page with the colors swapped? If yes, it is slop regardless
of how well it tokenizes. Recompose, don't repaint.
