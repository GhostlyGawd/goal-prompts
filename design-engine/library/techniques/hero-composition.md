# Technique — hero composition

How the board builds a hero (decode of arch-hall + arched-interior), as a
recipe:

1. **The world fills the frame** — full-bleed art (re-inked reference or dense
   scene), not an illustration floating in whitespace.
2. **Depth by repetition** — arches/shelves/tiles receding to a dark focal
   doorway slightly off-center.
3. **One glow** — the orange lamp, upper third, cord visible, nothing else
   glows.
4. **The panel carries the words** — content sits ON the art in a paper card
   with a 2px ink border and hard offset shadow (print, not blur), overlapping
   the frame edge by feel. Eyebrow (mono, letterspaced) → Fraunces headline
   with ONE italic cobalt phrase → serif sub with the → FILE.md glyph →
   solid-cobalt + ink-ghost buttons → mono statline.
5. **Nav floats on paper gradient** — readable over art via a paper fade, ink
   text, arcade mark left, cobalt CTA right.
6. **Grain over everything**; ink rule at the frame's base.

Checklist before shipping any hero: squint = board's world? one glow? panel
text all pre-gated for contrast? nav legible over the busiest strip of art?
works at 390px wide (panel goes full-width, art crops center)?
